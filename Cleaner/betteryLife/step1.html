Avoid Maximizing Your Charge Cycles.

As per information from Apple, iPhones tend to retain only up to 80% of their original battery capacity after approximately 500 charge cycles. A charge cycle is completed when the entirety of the iPhone's battery is used up—for instance, using 50% yesterday and another 50% today.

How can count number of Battery Cycles:
  1. Head over to Settings > Privacy > Analytics & Improvements.
  2. Make sure Share iPhone & Watch Analytics is enabled.
  3. Tap on Analytics Data.
  4. Scroll down to log-aggregated-2023-01-16-xxxxxx.ips (notice the date in the file name along with the numbers, and pick the latest file from the list).
  5. Now comes the tedious part… you can either copy and paste this into the Notes app (scrolling to do the copy is a pain in the rear), or, what I do is I email it to myself and look at it on a laptop or desktop.
  6. In whatever app you've opened the file in, search for BatteryCycleCount, and that will give you your recharge cycles.

It's essential to note that maintaining the device at a constant full charge or allowing it to completely drain can have adverse effects on the iPhone's battery health. Therefore, it's advisable to maintain your device's battery level between 40% and 80% whenever possible to preserve the longevity of your iPhone's battery.
