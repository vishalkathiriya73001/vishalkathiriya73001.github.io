Low Power Mode, introduced several years ago, remains a top choice for users concerned about maximizing their iPhone's battery life. This mode is designed to reduce background activities, such as automatic downloads, and accelerates the dimming of the display after periods of inactivity.

To enable Low Power Mode:
  1. Open Settings.
  2. Scroll down and tap Battery.
  3. Tap the Low Power Mode toggle.
  4. Alternatively, you can activate it through Siri by simply asking, or by navigating to Settings > Battery and toggling the Low Power Mode switch.

When active, Low Power Mode is indicated by a yellow battery icon at the top of your iPhone's screen. Some users opt to keep Low Power Mode on continuously, but it's important to note that it needs to be re-enabled periodically, as it automatically turns off when the iPhone is charged.

Enabling Low Power Mode is a practical strategy to extend battery life, particularly during times when preserving power is essential.
