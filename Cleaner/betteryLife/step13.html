Turn Off Fitness Tracking

Your iPhone includes a motion coprocessor that detects changes in the accelerometer, gyroscope, barometer, and compass, and uses theses changes to measure and track your movement, elevation change, or other fitness activity.

To disable fitness tracking on iPhone:
  1. Open Settings.
  2. Tap on "Privacy."
  3. Select "Motion & Fitness."
  4. Toggle off the switch next to "Fitness Tracking."

By following these steps, users can deactivate fitness tracking, potentially conserving battery life, especially during periods when detailed motion and fitness data aren't required.
