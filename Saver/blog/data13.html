How you measure the effectiveness of your influencer marketing campaigns depends on your goals. In this article, we will list and explain the most popular metrics. Let's take a closer look at them.

1. Reach and Impressions

These metrics stand as pillars of influencer marketing measurement, providing invaluable insights into the scope and visibility of a campaign. Reach and impressions help us see how many people have seen the influencer's sponsored content and how often it's been displayed online.

Reach: Reach tells us the total number of unique users who have seen the sponsored content with your brand or product mentions. It shows us how many people could have come across the message.

Impressions: Impressions, on the other hand, count the total times the influencer's sponsored content has been displayed. It doesn't matter if the same person saw it multiple times; impressions give us an idea of how often the content has been shown. More impressions mean more exposure, making more people aware of the brand.

In simpler terms, reach is about the number of people who've seen the content, while Impressions are about how many times the content has been shown. Together, these metrics help us understand how well the influencer's sponsored content is reaching and resonating with the audience, giving insights into the overall impact of the influencer marketing campaign.

3. Follower Growth

Follower growth measures the increase in the number of people who choose to follow your brand's social media accounts after seeing your sponsored post on an influencer's account. More followers indicate a greater interest in what the influencer shares about your product or brand. Simultaneously, the content that the audience saw on your account also caught their interest. This implies that people not only appreciate what they see but also wish to stay connected for future content.

Keep in mind that the click-through rate (CTR) is usually higher during the initial placement, and with subsequent placements, it tends to decrease.

7. Brand Mentions

Tracking the number of times the brand is mentioned in the influencer's content is more suitable for campaigns focused on creating user-generated content. In traditional campaigns, the quantity of brand mentions depends solely on you. You'll get as many mentions as you pay for.

However, in the case of user-generated content campaigns, the number of mentions your brand receives speaks to the campaign's effectiveness and its viral potential. The more mentions you acquire, the more successful the influencer marketing campaign has been.

8. Sentiment Analysis

Analyzing sentiment, with rare exceptions, cannot be a standalone KPI for evaluating influencer marketing campaigns, but it is an important indicator that shows how the audience perceives your brand and product.

When conducting sentiment analysis, the tone of comments on the influencer's sponsored post is evaluated. A large number of positive comments indicate that the advertising message and your product are well-received by the audience. 

If the goal of your advertising campaign is to assess changes in the audience's perception of your brand, sentiment analysis becomes a key metric.

Sentiment analysis can be performed using social listening tools and within the campaign tracking features available on many influencer marketing platforms. It offers valuable insights into the overall sentiment surrounding your brand within the influencer's audience.

9. Brand Lift

Brand Lift is a metric that evaluates the impact of the influencer marketing campaign on crucial brand-related measures, such as brand awareness, perception, or consideration. To measure brand lift, you should compare these metrics before and after the advertising campaign to identify the positive changes attributed to the campaign.

Brand lift typically involves assessing the brand across various parameters, including:

  • Audience awareness

  • The shift in consumer attitude toward the brand before and after the campaign

  • Likelihood to recommend the brand to acquaintances

  • Purchase intent indicator

  • Brand preference indicator

There isn't a strict set of metrics measured within the evaluation of brand lift; you decide which indicators are important for you.

Brand lift is typically measured through surveys and interviews among both the exposed group and the control group and is conducted by specialized agencies. Additionally, there is an option to set up a brand lift analysis on YouTube.

12. Earned Media Value (EMV)

Earned Media Value (EMV) is a metric used to quantify the value of publicity gained through promotional efforts other than paid advertising. This includes mentions, shares, likes, or any form of recommendation that comes from sources outside the company's direct payments or advertisements. EMV is often used to measure the effectiveness of marketing strategies, particularly in social media, public relations, and influencer marketing campaigns.

The idea behind EMV is to assign a dollar value to the exposure and engagement a brand receives from these non-paid sources, making it easier to compare the return on investment (ROI) with that of paid media. Calculating EMV involves using industry-standard rates for advertising space or impressions, engagement rates, and other metrics to estimate what the equivalent exposure would have cost if it were paid for.

It also highlights the importance of building strong relationships with customers, influencers, and other stakeholders who can amplify the brand's message without direct financial incentives.

Conclusion

To understand how well your influencer marketing is working, it's important to use different measurements. These include looking at how many people see and interact with your posts (Reach, Impressions, and Engagement Rate), how interested people are in following your brand (Follower Growth), and how much it costs to get their attention (CPM). Also, check if people are taking actions like buying or signing up (Conversion Rate), how your campaign compares to others (Share of Voice), and how much value you get from non-paid promotions (Earned Media Value). Finally, knowing your Return on Investment (ROI) tells you if the money you're spending is bringing in profit.

This guide shows that successful influencer marketing is not just about running campaigns, but also about carefully checking their results. By choosing the right things to measure and learning from the outcomes, brands can improve their strategies, manage their budgets better, and build stronger relationships with their audience.
